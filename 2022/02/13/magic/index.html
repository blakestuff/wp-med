Magic

I’m proposing changing the way magic works in the 5th edition D&D to better fit with a medieval lower magic setting. I’m proposing breaking the magic into several categories, natural and unnatural magic. And possibly divine and earth(?) magic.

I’m also limiting the spell using classes to wizard alone. Only wizards are born with a connection to the magical weave that exists to facilitate all magic within the world. Non wizards can use magic through a focus connected to the weave by a pre-existing thread. This previously connected thread could salvaged from the body of a wizard, or other naturally magical creature such as unicorn or dragon.

The ‘Item’ would retain some of the magical potential of its host. Its potential capacity could not be exceeded and would cap the power of its user to that of the host. Thus a ‘item’ taken from the body of a 5th level wizard could not exceed the spell levels of a 5th level wizard.

Natural magic

True wizards are born with a wide range of spell casting potential, they instinctively know many magic effects and powers. Their ability to cast spells is based on their inherent will power which will increase as they gain experience.

Unnatural magic

Over the millennia wizards have pushed the boundaries of spell casting and frustrated with the scope of natural magic sought to invent new and more dangerous magic. These spells must be learned in order to be cast and they are complex in structure and require certain focusing items. A mage may not have more than X of these spells. How does it work? So ideas so far:

Wizard – gets all natural magic spells to choose from each level. they don’t get any unnatural magic. All weave magic is arcane magic, do I need a name for it?

Everyone gets simple weapons and light armour by default – even wizards.

Any non wizard can access arcane/weave magic through a weave connection (thread). This gives them access to certain spells known to the magic using host. How much magic , which spells etc – to be determined. In order for the non wizard to use the magic they must study arcane powers – become a weave mage? Follow the multi-class rules.

Cleric – prays for magic from a long list of spells. No arcane spells. Who do they pray to, gods of course. Why is divine magic different from weave magic? Don’t know. Maybe only one god? Father? Sky god.

Druid – Who do they pray to? nature Hmm, too much like the weave. Nature spirits. Ancestor spirits? Nature gods, probably only one god? Mother? Earth mother.

Paladin and ranger – children of mother and father gods? Warrior and hunteress.

Third child sacrificed herself starlight. Formed the weave.

Druids and rangers get an extra Feat at 1st level to compensate for loss of arcane spells. Blessing of the earth mother.

Might want to consider what to do about the spare few bard and warlock spells. There aren’t many. maybe add them to wizard learnable spells for (bard) the storytelling travelling magician? or (warlock) magic a from another age and time.

Druids and clerics worship angels or avatars of the old gods creators that have faded from memory or stepped back from interfering directly in the world. Wizards draw their power directly fro the weave, the earth and air around them so can cast any magic at will. Druids and clerics take time to petition their gods for intervention through magic and must prove devotion through prayer and sacrifice.

The young gods worshipped by people today are but avatars of the creators, heroes, angels and demons. Their ascension was precipitated by a lifetime of devotion and sacrifice in the name of their own beliefs. There could be many layers of gods between the first ones and the current pantheon of celestial deities.

Natural magic requires no physical component the only restrictions being the number of spells a wizard is able to cast daily, this equates to effort spent through concentration and takes it’s toll bodily, spiritually and mentally.

How does a person get hold of a weave threaded item. Dead wizards are the best source.

Also dead weave touched creatures? Weave touched creatures some mythical creatures have magical powers. Rumours of giants who can control the weather snd devils with fire at their command.

Wizards are born with a connection to the weave, they gain full control of their powers at around 12 – 15 years of age. In game terms a wizard can use natural cantrips from 12 – 15 onwards, level 1 spells from 15 – 18 years of age. Wizards are extremely rare and completely asymmetrical in my D&D world.

The dead body of a 18 year old wizard will afford the opportunity for a range of 1st level spells and cantrips. Going to have to make this random unless there is a good reason to select spells. How many? maybe the spell slots worth? + int bonus? So roughly 4 cantrips and maybe 4 1st level spells. Maybe level + int bonus in spells of each level?

How do you harvest this link to the weave? It is a physical thing. Yes. Bones? Flesh, blood, organs, brain seems likely or heart. Skull. – there are rituals where the link can be transferred from the skull to an item.

Idea, no two dead wizards are the same. It will require a detect magic to isolate the thread linked part of the wizards corpse that retains the thread.

Transferring the weave linked body part into a magic item involves a ceremony(?) where the strength of the thread is maintained but the spells are stripped away in favour of a the intended magical property.

Arcane magic was lost to the gods during the ‘god wars’ when the ‘god of magic’ died and fell to the land and was absorbed. Divine magic is all that is left in ‘Heaven’. Ancient ones challenged gods with arcane magic – netherill.
